Biology

Biological Molecules

Main nutrients include carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, vitamins, minerals, and water.

Digestive System

The alimentary canal includes the mouth, digestion, small intestine, large intestine, and anus.

Defence Against Infectious Disease

White blood cells defend the body: lymphocytes produce antibodies, phagocytes engulf pathogens and help build immunity.

Chemistry

Atomic Structure

Atoms contain equal numbers of protons and electrons, so they are neutral.

Ions and Ionic Compounds

Metals lose electrons to form cations, while non-metals gain electrons to form anions.

Acids and Bases

Acids react with bases to form salt and water; an alkali is a soluble base.

Geography

Tectonic Hazards

The Earth has four main layers and tectonic plates are responsible for earthquakes and volcanoes.

Maths

Key Maths Structure

Linear graphs, simultaneous equations, expansion and factorisation, quadratics, and algebraic fractions are the main revision areas here.

Linear Graphs and Simultaneous Equations

Horizontal lines have equations y = c, vertical lines have equations x = a, and simultaneous equations can be solved by elimination, substitution, or graphical method.

Expansion and Special Products

Use the distributive law to expand, combine like terms, and remember identities such as (a + b)^2, (a - b)^2, and a^2 - b^2.

Quadratic Equations and Graphs

Quadratic equations can be solved by factorisation and graphs have a turning point and a line of symmetry.

Algebraic Fractions

Factorise first, cancel common factors only, and use the LCM or LCD when adding or subtracting algebraic fractions.
